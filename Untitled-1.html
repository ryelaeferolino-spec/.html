Community Problem Identification

Floods are one of the most common community problem. They can cause severe damage, destroy crops, and even spread diseases. It harms people and the environment, which leads to economic losses and health risks.

Research Methods

Community Problem Qualitative Quantitative Mixed Method
Flooding Interview residents and assess how frequent flooding affects them. Identify the number of people that are affected by the flooding and determine which area is the most prone to flooding. Conduct surveys and interviews to gather both numerical data of the residents and personal experiences regarding on the impacts of flooding.
Improper waste management Interview people to understand how they manage and what's their practices in handling waste, and the challenges they go through in managing their waste. Survey residents and determine how many practice proper waste segregation. Conducting surveys and interviews to gather numerical data and residents experiences about waste management.
Traffic Congestion Interview residents about how traffic affects their daily routine. Determine the number of commuters affected by traffic congestion and the extensof its impact on their daily travel. Survey and interview commuters to determine the number of commuters affected by traffic congestion and understand its impact on their daily travel.
